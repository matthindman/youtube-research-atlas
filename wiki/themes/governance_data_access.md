# Platform Governance and Researcher Data Access

> Provisional multi-source state on the way to the Batch 5 governance
> refresh. This page will be rewritten once the full seven-source
> governance corpus is assembled on the dedicated refresh branch.

## Why This Theme Matters

This theme now combines field-level access constraints, creator-side
governance complaints, and comparative evidence about external public
pressure and governance change. [🤖]

## Current Consensus

- Researchers and creators both face major information deficits when
  evaluating YouTube governance from the outside. [🤖]
- Creator publicity is often used as an accountability tactic when
  formal recourse feels weak, incomplete, or unevenly distributed.
  [🤖]
- Comparative evidence suggests that public scrutiny and governance
  change can affect visible platform outcomes, but those findings are
  not the same thing as transparent internal governance. [🤖]

## Main Disagreements

The present source set still disagrees more about what governance
signals mean than about whether those signals exist: creator callouts,
user-generated accountability, media pressure, and ownership change
may all matter, but their causal leverage remains uncertain. [🤖]

## Methodological Reasons for Disagreement

These sources capture different governance layers: field-level access
constraints, creator discourse, comparative public-policy traces, and
comparative governance-change effects. They reveal visible governance
dynamics more directly than internal decision-making processes. [🤖]

## Measures and Variables Used in the Literature

- Norton and Shapiro track platform coverage, method mix, and
  researcher-reported access and infrastructure bottlenecks. [🤖]
- The Hallinan/Reynolds papers code creator identity, accountability
  targets, copyright-claim types, complaints, and proposed remedies.
  [🤖]
- Marchal et al. measure news volume, negative sentiment,
  public-facing policy change, and comparative platform pressure.
  [🤖]
- Ozturan et al. measure URL-sharing patterns, NewsGuard scores,
  interrupted time-series shifts, and market-share changes across
  credibility buckets. [🤖]

## What This Means for Our Project

- Governance claims need explicit period labels and clear separation
  between creator-side evidence, comparative evidence, and platform-
  side policy evidence. [🤖]
- The dedicated governance refresh should absorb the DSA access source
  before this theme is used for manuscript-level synthesis. [🤖]

## Open Holes / Next Sources to Acquire

- The DSA access-policy source still needs to be integrated. [🤖]
- The current corpus still lacks direct evidence about when creator
  publicity produces durable policy change rather than case-specific
  relief. [🤖]
