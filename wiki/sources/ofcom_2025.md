# Ofcom (2025) - Online Nation Report 2025

## Full Citation

Ofcom. 2025. *Online Nation Report 2025*. Published December 10.

## One-Sentence Contribution

This UK regulatory industry report provides a non-peer-reviewed but
independent 2025 baseline showing that YouTube remains one of the
largest services in UK online life, with 94% adult reach, 51 minutes of
daily adult use, and near-universal reach among children aged 8-14 (pp.
4, 34-36, 91-92). [🤖]

## Research Question

This is not a single-paper research design. It is an Ofcom monitoring
report that tracks the UK's online landscape, platform use, online
experiences, and children's digital lives using audience measurement,
survey, and passive-measurement sources (pp. 4, 111-114). [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** Ipsos iris online audience measurement for UK internet
  users, Ofcom's Online Experiences Tracker, and the Children's Passive
  Online Measurement study based on tracked device use (pp. 111-114).
  [🤖]
- **Sample:** The YouTube-specific figures used here come from UK online
  adults aged 18+ in May 2025 and from a UK representative sample of
  692 online children aged 8-14 tracked over 28 days between November
  2024 and March 2025 (pp. 34-36, 90-92, 114). [🤖]
- **Geography:** United Kingdom only; the report is not a global or
  cross-national YouTube baseline (pp. 4, 17, 34, 111-114). [🤖]
- **Period:** Adult platform metrics are anchored to May 2025, while
  the children's passive-measurement window runs from November 2024
  through March 2025 (pp. 4, 34-36, 90, 114). [🤖]

## Methods

The report is descriptive rather than causal. It combines third-party
audience measurement and Ofcom survey infrastructure to estimate reach,
time spent, app use, and service composition across the UK online
landscape (pp. 4, 17, 24, 111-114). [🤖]

For children, Ofcom uses passive monitoring software installed on
devices used by a representative panel of 8-14-year-olds, which yields
service-level time-spent and reach estimates but only for the tracked
device categories rather than for every possible screen or console (pp.
90-92, 114). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. In May 2025, YouTube reached 94% of UK online adults and averaged 51
   minutes per day, making it the most-used Alphabet-owned service and
   the top social-media/video-sharing platform in the report's UK adult
   landscape (pp. 4, 34-35). [🤖]
2. YouTube also remained especially strong among younger UK adults: 84%
   of 18-34 smartphone users visited the app in May 2025, and 18-34
   YouTube visitors spent about 1 hour 28 minutes per day on the
   service (pp. 24, 36). [🤖]
3. Among UK children aged 8-14, YouTube and Google Search had the
   highest reach, with 96% of children using YouTube, and children in
   that age range spent an average of 48 minutes per day on it (pp.
   91-92). [🤖]
4. Ofcom's news chapter treats YouTube as one of the social
   intermediaries inside the UK online news environment, underscoring
   that the platform matters not only as entertainment infrastructure
   but also as part of how news reaches audiences (p. 45). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This is an `industry_report`, not peer-reviewed scholarship. It is
useful for current UK platform-use baselines, but it cannot support
causal claims or stand in for scholarly consensus about YouTube's
effects or mechanisms (pp. 111-114). [🤖]

The findings are UK-specific. They should not be generalized to the
United States or to YouTube globally without additional evidence (pp.
4, 17, 34, 111-114). [🤖]

The adult time-spent figures exclude TV-set viewing, and the child
passive-measurement system excludes some device classes such as games
consoles, so the reported YouTube totals are substantial but still not
full-screen totals across every viewing environment (pp. 4, 35, 90-92).
[🤖]

The report does not isolate Shorts as a separate measurement layer in
its YouTube sections, so it cannot answer whether YouTube's UK reach or
time growth is being driven mainly by long-form video, Shorts, or other
formats (pp. 34-36, 91-92). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best treated as a 2025 UK descriptive baseline. It is
valuable for showing how large YouTube remains in current national
platform use, but it does not provide a time series deep enough to
describe long-run structural change and it should not be read as a
global platform denominator (pp. 4, 17, 34-36, 90-92). [🤖]
