# Newman et al. (2025) - Mapping News Creators and Influencers

## Full Citation

Newman, Nic, Amy Ross Arguedas, Mitali Mukherjee, and Richard Fletcher. 2025. "Mapping News Creators and Influencers in Social and Video Networks." Reuters Institute for the Study of Journalism. https://doi.org/10.60625/risj-44pf-1k13

## One-Sentence Contribution

Using audience-side open-text survey data from 24 countries, the report maps who news creators are on social and video networks and shows that YouTube-centered news influence is especially associated with commentary-driven creator ecosystems (pp. 8-15). [✓]

## Research Question

How and why are news creators and influencers gaining attention across 24 countries, which types of creators are most prominent on social and video networks, and where is YouTube playing the biggest role in that shift? (pp. 5, 8-15) [✓]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** Open-text responses from the Reuters Institute Digital News Report surveys, supplemented with desk research, country expert review, follower counts, and manual recoding of named individuals and brands (p. 8). [🤖]
- **Sample:** Respondents who said they used Facebook, X, YouTube, Instagram, Snapchat, or TikTok for news were asked about one randomly selected network; the analysis then counted mentions from the 2024 and 2025 survey waves across 24 countries (pp. 8, 12). [🤖]
- **Geography:** Twenty-four countries across Europe, the Americas, Asia-Pacific, and Africa, including the United States, Brazil, India, Thailand, Kenya, and others (pp. 5, 8). [🤖]
- **Period:** The report pools 2024 and 2025 survey responses, with a few markets relying on 2025 only where the earlier question was not asked (p. 8). [🤖]

## Methods

The authors use an audience-centric method built from open-text survey fields, OpenRefine and clustering tools, ChatGPT-assisted recoding, manual checking, and country-expert review to identify the most-mentioned creators, build a typology, and compare country-level ecosystems (pp. 8-11). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. Commentary is the most frequently mentioned creator category in the data, and on YouTube it is often cheap to produce, personality-led, and only weakly constrained by traditional journalistic norms (p. 10). [✓]
2. Across 24 countries, the top creator/influencer lists are overwhelmingly male: 85% of the top 15 in each country are men (p. 14). [✓]
3. In most countries attention focuses on domestic creators, but English-speaking markets show substantial cross-border import of right-leaning US commentators such as Joe Rogan, Tucker Carlson, and Ben Shapiro via YouTube (p. 13). [✓]
4. YouTube is the most important platform for news creators overall, though the relative strength of creators versus traditional media varies widely by country and network (pp. 12-15, 80). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

The analysis is built from self-reported survey behavior, which may diverge from actual exposure because of recall problems and other response biases (p. 8). [🤖]

The creator typology is intentionally provisional because the dividing lines between journalism, commentary, explanation, entertainment, and politics are often "extremely fuzzy" (pp. 9-11). [🤖]

Rank order within country top lists should be treated as indicative rather than definitive because the open-text method, recoding decisions, and sample margins all introduce uncertainty near the cut-off points (pp. 8, 21). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

The report captures a 2024-2025 audience snapshot of creator attention rather than long-run creator careers or direct content exposure logs. It is best used for cross-market orientation and taxonomy design, not as a stable estimate of enduring creator influence (pp. 8, 21, 80). [🤖]
