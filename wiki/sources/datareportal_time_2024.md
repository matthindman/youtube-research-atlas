# Kemp (2024) - The Time We Spend on Social Media

## One-Sentence Contribution

This DataReportal industry article is useful because it shows how
cross-platform dominance claims depend on the metric: the piece ranks
YouTube first on total Android-app time spent while ranking TikTok
first on average time per user, making it a good non-peer-reviewed
source for metric-sensitive platform comparisons rather than for any
simple "largest platform" conclusion (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user).
[🤖]

## Research Question

The article asks how much time people spend on social platforms overall
and which platforms dominate under different usage metrics such as total
time and average time per user (§ Introduction; § Total.time; § Average.time.per.user).
[🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** The article synthesizes GWI survey estimates of social media
  time and data.ai app-use intelligence on total and average time per
  user (§ Introduction; § Total.time; § Average.time.per.user). [🤖]
- **Sample:** The platform rankings highlighted here depend on Android
  app activity and third-quarter 2023 app-use data rather than on
  full-device telemetry or audited company disclosures (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user).
  [🤖]
- **Geography:** The article presents a global comparative frame, but
  the app-based rankings inherit the limitations of third-party mobile
  measurement rather than a full worldwide census (§ Introduction; § Total.time).
  [🤖]
- **Period:** Published January 31, 2024 using app-time estimates
  centered on late-2023 activity (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user).
  [🤖]

## Methods

This is an industry synthesis article. It combines public summary
statistics from GWI and data.ai to describe aggregate social-media time
and platform rankings, rather than presenting an original transparent
research dataset (§ Introduction; § Total.time). [🤖]

The platform comparison is explicitly metric-dependent. The article
distinguishes total time, which favors large installed bases, from
average time per user, which favors more intensive use among a smaller
user base (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. DataReportal says YouTube "tops the ranking by total time spent,"
   with Android app users spending more than 10 trillion minutes per
   month on the platform (§ Total.time). [🤖]
2. The same section says YouTube commands more than twice as much total
   user time as TikTok and about 40% more time than Facebook on this
   Android-based measure (§ Total.time). [🤖]
3. The article simultaneously reports that TikTok, not YouTube, leads
   on average time per user, while YouTube ranks second at 28 hours and
   5 minutes per month per Android user (§ Average.time.per.user). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This is an `industry_report`, not peer-reviewed scholarship. It is
useful for cross-platform orientation, but it cannot support causal
claims or stand in for direct independent measurement of YouTube's
internal attention allocation (§ Introduction; § Total.time). [🤖]

The headline ranking depends on Android app activity and app-store
classification choices. The article itself notes that TikTok and
YouTube fall under "entertainment" rather than "social media" in some
classifications, which makes apparently simple platform rankings
method-dependent (§ Introduction; § Total.time). [🤖]

Because total time and average time per user produce different winners,
the source should be used to qualify comparative claims rather than to
collapse them into one generic statement that YouTube leads on every
attention metric (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best used as a 2024 public comparison of app-based time
metrics. It can support softened statements that YouTube led some late-
2023 public time-spent rankings, but it should not be treated as a
timeless or comprehensive denominator for all platform attention
metrics (§ Total.time; § Average.time.per.user). [🤖]
