# Ozturan et al. (2025) - Declining Information Quality Under New Platform Governance

## Full Citation

Ozturan, Burak, Alexi Quintana-Mathe, Nir Grinberg, Katherine Ognyanova, and David Lazer. 2025. "Declining Information Quality Under New Platform Governance." *Harvard Kennedy School Misinformation Review* 6(3). https://doi.org/10.37016/mr-2020-176

## One-Sentence Contribution

Using a Twitter panel and a global Decahose sample around the October 27, 2022 Twitter/X takeover, the paper shows that ownership and governance changes were associated with a modest but statistically significant decline in the quality of information shared on the platform (pp. 1-7). [🤖]

## Research Question

How did overall information quality and the market share of low- and high-quality sources change on Twitter/X following the October 27, 2022 ownership transition? (p. 1) [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** A fixed Twitter panel of registered U.S. voters, a global 10% Decahose sample, and NewsGuard domain-reliability scores matched to shared URLs (pp. 1-4, 13-14). [🤖]
- **Sample:** The analysis covers tweets from January 1, 2022 through April 30, 2023; across both datasets there are 18,520,788,890 total tweets, including 481,575,162 URL-containing Decahose tweets and 8,196,662 URL-containing panel tweets (pp. 13-14). [🤖]
- **Geography:** The panel captures U.S. voter behavior, while the Decahose reflects global platform-wide circulation including organizations and automated accounts (pp. 2-4, 13-14). [🤖]
- **Period:** The study compares pre- and post-acquisition information quality around the October 27, 2022 ownership transition, with usable data ending in May 2023 because of X policy changes (pp. 1-4). [🤖]

## Methods

The authors match URL domains in both datasets to NewsGuard reliability scores, compute monthly average information quality, and use interrupted time-series models plus robustness checks to estimate immediate and trend changes after the ownership transition, then decompose those changes by market-share shifts across quality buckets (pp. 4-10, 13-14). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. Information quality declined in both datasets after the ownership transition: the Twitter panel shows an immediate 1.45-point drop and the Decahose a 1.39-point drop, followed by sustained downward trends in both series (pp. 4-7). [🤖]
2. The decline is driven by a changing composition of shared content, with lower-credibility sources gaining market share while higher-credibility sources contract in both absolute and relative terms (pp. 7-10). [🤖]
3. The effect sizes are modest on a 0-100 scale and high-quality sources still dominate most shared content, but the authors argue that even small sustained declines can matter for the platform's information environment (pp. 2-4, 7-10). [🤖]
4. The authors interpret these shifts as evidence that ownership change and subsequent governance adjustments can alter information quality in a sociotechnical system, even if the exact mechanism remains uncertain (pp. 1-4, 6-7). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

The paper is about Twitter/X rather than YouTube, so it should be used only as comparative governance evidence rather than direct evidence about YouTube's information environment (pp. 1-4). [🤖]

The study lacks exposure data, so it cannot determine whether users actually saw more low-quality content in their feeds as opposed to merely sharing more of it (pp. 3-4). [🤖]

Data access ended in May 2023 because of X policy changes, so the paper cannot show whether the observed decline persisted, stabilized, or reversed later (p. 4). [🤖]

Information quality is measured at the domain level rather than the individual-story level, which may miss substantial within-domain variation in credibility (p. 4). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This paper is tightly bounded to the immediate pre/post period around the October 27, 2022 Twitter/X ownership change. It is most useful as comparative evidence that governance regime changes can matter for information quality, not as a current or YouTube-specific claim about the post-2025 platform environment (pp. 1-4, 6-10). [🤖]
