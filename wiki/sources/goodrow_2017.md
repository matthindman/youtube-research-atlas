# Goodrow (2017) - A Billion Hours

## Full Citation

Goodrow, Cristos. 2017. "You know what's cool? A billion hours."
*YouTube Blog*. February 27. Retrieved 2026-04-17 from
https://blog.youtube/news-and-events/you-know-whats-cool-billion-hours/

_Citation convention: this web article is archived as `.htm` / `.mhtml`,
so citations use section locators rather than fixed page numbers._

## One-Sentence Contribution

This YouTube Blog post is the clearest platform-of-record source for
the company's February 2017 claim that viewers were watching a billion
hours of YouTube content per day, which makes it useful for stage-
setting about platform scale but not for independent comparative rank
claims (Introduction). [🤖]

## Research Question

This is not a research article. It is a milestone announcement in which
YouTube explains why it shifted from raw view counts toward watch time
and celebrates having reached a claimed one-billion-hours-per-day watch
time threshold (Introduction). [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** Platform-authored milestone statement about daily watch
  time, framed through YouTube's own internal metrics and product
  rationale rather than a public methods appendix (Introduction). [🤖]
- **Sample:** The post does not describe a formal sample; it presents a
  global platform aggregate claimed by YouTube (Introduction). [🤖]
- **Geography:** Global rather than country-specific, expressed as
  "people around the world" watching YouTube content (Introduction).
  [🤖]
- **Period:** The milestone is announced on February 27, 2017 and
  reflects pre-Shorts YouTube watch-time scale in that period
  (Introduction). [🤖]

## Methods

The post does not present formal methods. It is a platform-authored
communication that links YouTube's internal shift toward watch time as
a success metric with a claimed global milestone of one billion hours
watched per day (Introduction). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. YouTube says viewers were watching a billion hours of content per
   day by early 2017, which is the post's central milestone claim
   (Introduction). [🤖]
2. The post retrospectively frames YouTube's earlier product decision
   to prioritize watch time over raw views as the metric that better
   captures whether viewers enjoyed a video (Introduction). [🤖]
3. The announcement presents the milestone as a global aggregate driven
   by many usage modes, including music, news, personalities, and trend
   following, rather than by a single content vertical (Introduction).
   [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This source is `platform_documentation`, not independent scholarship.
It is usable for YouTube's own claimed milestone and self-description of
metric priorities, but not as external evidence that the figure was
independently measured or directly comparable to every other digital
platform (Introduction). [🤖]

The archived `.htm` and `.mhtml` files appear to be duplicate captures
of the same blog post rather than distinct statements, so this card
treats them as supplementary archives for one source. [🤖]

The post provides no public denominator, no methods appendix, and no
comparative benchmark against other platforms, so it should not be used
to support stronger claims than the milestone itself (Introduction).
  [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best treated as a February 2017 platform milestone in a
pre-Shorts, pre-pandemic YouTube environment. It motivates why the
platform matters at scale, but it is not a current baseline and should
not be read as evidence about later watch-time distributions or post-
2020 platform structure (Introduction). [🤖]
