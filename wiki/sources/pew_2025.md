# Gottfried and Park (2025) - Americans' Social Media Use 2025

## Full Citation

Gottfried, Jeffrey, and Eugenie Park. 2025. *Americans' Social Media
Use 2025*. Pew Research Center. Published November 20.

## One-Sentence Contribution

This U.S.-specific Pew industry report provides a non-peer-reviewed 2025
baseline showing that YouTube remains the most widely used platform
among U.S. adults at 84% reach, with broad use across age groups and
48% daily use overall (pp. 4, 6, 9-11). [🤖]

## Research Question

The report tracks which social media platforms U.S. adults use, how
that platform mix has changed over time, how use varies across
demographic groups, and how frequently Americans use a subset of those
platforms in 2025 (pp. 4-11). [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** Two Pew survey programs: the 2025 National Public Opinion
  Reference Survey for platform adoption and a separate American Trends
  Panel survey for daily-use frequency on selected platforms (pp. 3,
  9-11, 13-14). [🤖]
- **Sample:** The adoption estimates come from 5,022 U.S. adults
  surveyed from February 5 to June 18, 2025, while the daily-use
  estimates come from 5,123 U.S. adults surveyed from February 24 to
  March 2, 2025 (pp. 3-4, 9, 13-14). [🤖]
- **Geography:** United States only; the report is not a cross-national
  or global YouTube baseline (pp. 3-4, 13-14). [🤖]
- **Period:** The report captures 2025 self-reported platform use and a
  separate early-2025 daily-use snapshot rather than long-run observed
  behavior logs (pp. 3-5, 9-11). [🤖]

## Methods

Pew uses nationally sampled survey data rather than platform telemetry.
The adoption estimates rely on address-based sampling with web, mail,
and phone administration, while the daily-use estimates come from the
American Trends Panel using online and phone interviews (pp. 3, 9, 13-
14). [🤖]

The report is descriptive rather than causal. It compares self-reported
platform use across time and demographic groups, and it warns that the
2023 mode shift complicates direct comparison to older phone-only waves
(pp. 3, 5, 13-14). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. YouTube remains the most widely used platform among U.S. adults in
   2025, with 84% reporting use, ahead of Facebook at 71% and well
   ahead of Instagram, TikTok, and WhatsApp (p. 4). [🤖]
2. While TikTok, Instagram, WhatsApp, and Reddit continued to gain
   users since 2021, Pew reports that YouTube and Facebook remained at
   the top with relatively stable overall adoption shares (p. 5). [🤖]
3. YouTube is one of only two platforms used by majorities in every age
   group, and 48% of U.S. adults report using it daily, including 66%
   of adults ages 18-29 (pp. 6, 9-11). [🤖]
4. Daily YouTube use varies across demographic groups but remains broad:
   Pew reports 61% daily use among Hispanic adults, 56% among Black
   adults, 70% among Asian adults represented by English speakers, and
   53% among lower-income adults (p. 11). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This is an `industry_report`, not peer-reviewed scholarship. It is
useful for U.S. platform-adoption and frequency baselines, but it cannot
support causal claims or claims about YouTube's content, recommendation
mechanisms, or effects (pp. 3-5, 13-14). [🤖]

The report is U.S.-specific and survey-based, so it should not be
treated as a global platform baseline or as direct behavioral telemetry
(pp. 3-4, 13-14). [🤖]

Pew explicitly warns that its 2023 mode shift complicates longer-run
trend comparisons with earlier phone-based waves, so apparent change
since 2021 should be interpreted cautiously (pp. 3, 5). [🤖]

Some subgroup estimates are narrower than they appear. In particular,
Asian-adult estimates are representative of English speakers only, and
daily-use patterns are based on a separate survey from the adoption
estimates (pp. 8, 11). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best used as a U.S. 2025 descriptive baseline. It shows
that YouTube still sits at the top of the U.S. social-platform
landscape, but it does not reveal what Americans watch on YouTube, how
they arrive there, or how the platform compares to other countries (pp.
4-11). [🤖]
