## One-Sentence Contribution

This DataReportal industry article provides a non-peer-reviewed global
comparison suggesting that YouTube was the most-used social platform at
the start of 2025, but the ranking rests on third-party Android app-use
estimates and explicitly excludes China, so it should be used as
qualified stage-setting rather than as a definitive platform census
(§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users). [🤖]

## Research Question

The article asks which social platforms are "top" in 2025 under
different metrics, emphasizing that company-reported user numbers are
not directly comparable and therefore requiring a synthesis of third-
party sources instead (§ Introduction; § Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** The article synthesizes third-party platform-use sources,
  especially data.ai Android app-use estimates, Meta ad-tool figures,
  and Statcounter device-share data (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
  [🤖]
- **Sample:** The headline YouTube ranking is anchored to Android
  activity in November 2024 rather than to full multi-device telemetry
  or audited platform counts (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
  [🤖]
- **Geography:** The article presents a near-global comparison but says
  the ranking excludes China and relies on Android-dominant mobile
  markets (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users). [🤖]
- **Period:** Published February 5, 2025 using late-2024 third-party
  activity estimates rather than a direct 2025 behavioral census (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
  [🤖]

## Methods

This is an industry synthesis rather than a standalone research design.
It compares multiple proprietary and third-party measurement sources to
rank platforms under different definitions of "top," while warning that
platform self-reports are not directly comparable (§ Introduction; § Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]

The YouTube claim depends heavily on Android app activity. The article
argues that Android covers enough of the global smartphone base to be
informative, but it does not provide a full multi-device, cross-platform
methods appendix or raw-data audit trail (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]

## Main Findings

1. DataReportal states that "YouTube is the most used social media
   platform at the start of 2025," making it one of the clearest public
   non-peer-reviewed sources for that softened comparative claim (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
   [🤖]
2. The article says data.ai's Android-based intelligence puts
   YouTube's active user base roughly 16% above WhatsApp, while Facebook,
   Instagram, and TikTok rank below YouTube on that measure (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
   [🤖]
3. The author explicitly warns that the ranking excludes China and that
   Apple privacy restrictions make iPhone behavior harder to track,
   which narrows what the comparison can really establish (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
   [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This is an `industry_report`, not peer-reviewed scholarship. It is
useful for high-level cross-platform orientation, but it cannot support
causal claims, content claims, or claims about YouTube's internal
mechanisms (§ Introduction; § Top.social.platform.by.active.users). [🤖]

The article is not a clean global census. Its headline comparison relies
on Android app activity, excludes China, and synthesizes vendor and
third-party numbers with different underlying methods (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]

The piece compares "use" across platforms rather than direct attention
or watch-time totals, so it should not be cited as evidence that YouTube
unequivocally leads every possible metric of platform importance (§ Introduction; § Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best used as a 2025 stage-setting article about public
platform rankings. It can support softened claims that YouTube remains a
top platform in contemporary comparative baselines, but it should not be
treated as a stable, audited denominator for the whole platform (§ Top.social.platform.by.active.users).
[🤖]
