# European Commission (2025) - DSA Researcher Access Measures

## Full Citation

European Commission. 2025. "New Measures Unlock Access to Data from Largest Online Platforms to Support Research." *Shaping Europe's Digital Future*. European Commission.

## One-Sentence Contribution

This Commission announcement states that, as of October 29, 2025, new Digital Services Act rules create a qualified-researcher route to request previously unavailable data from very large online platforms and search engines in order to study systemic risks. [🤖]

## Research Question

This is not a research article. It is a policy announcement explaining the new DSA data-access mechanism, the role of Digital Services Coordinators, and the kinds of systemic-risk research the mechanism is meant to support. [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** An official European Commission announcement about the delegated act on data access under the DSA, including links to the delegated act, DSA background materials, and lists of designated VLOPs/VLOSEs. [🤖]
- **Sample:** The document does not present an empirical sample; it applies to qualified researchers seeking access to data from very large online platforms and search engines. [🤖]
- **Geography:** European Union member states and the DSA regime governing designated very large online platforms and very large online search engines. [🤖]
- **Period:** The new access route is described as taking effect on October 29, 2025. [🤖]

## Methods

The document does not use research methods. It summarizes the delegated-act framework, the eligibility review by Digital Services Coordinators, and the legal obligation of covered platforms to comply when all criteria are met. [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. As of October 29, 2025, qualified researchers may request access to previously unavailable data from very large online platforms and search engines under the DSA. [🤖]
2. Access is conditioned on a strict assessment by Digital Services Coordinators, which are responsible for evaluating whether researchers and projects satisfy the legal criteria. [🤖]
3. The announcement frames the mechanism as a way to study systemic risks linked to platform systems, including recommender systems, illegal content, financial scams, and negative mental-health effects. [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

The document is `platform_documentation`, not a research study: it announces a legal mechanism and a procedural pathway, but it does not present original empirical analysis. [🤖]

The announcement does not show whether the access route will work smoothly in practice, how often requests will be granted, or how useful the released data will be for YouTube research once the regime is live. [🤖]

The text provides only a high-level summary of the process, so researchers would still need the delegated act and later implementation guidance to answer detailed procedural questions. [🤖]
