# Nielsen (2025) - Media Distributor Gauge: YouTube July 2025

## One-Sentence Contribution

This Nielsen industry article provides a device-specific U.S. baseline
showing that YouTube captured 13.4% of television-screen viewing in July
2025, which is useful for narrowly scoped stage-setting about U.S.
living-room attention but should not be mistaken for an all-device or
global YouTube share (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

## Research Question

The article reports the July 2025 Media Distributor Gauge results,
asking which media distributors captured the largest share of total U.S.
television-screen viewing during that interval (§ Introduction). [🤖]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** Nielsen's Media Distributor Gauge, which aggregates
  broadcast, cable, and streaming consumption occurring through a
  television screen (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge). [🤖]
- **Sample:** The article reports July 2025 distributor shares rather
  than a user-level research sample, with the interval running from June
  30, 2025 through July 27, 2025 (§ Introduction). [🤖]
- **Geography:** United States only (§ About.The.Gauge). [🤖]
- **Period:** July 2025 TV viewing, measured on Nielsen's broadcast-
  calendar interval of June 30-July 27, 2025 (§ Introduction). [🤖]

## Methods

This is an industry measurement summary, not a transparent academic
paper. The article reports Nielsen's monthly Gauge figures for total TV-
screen consumption across broadcast, cable, and streaming distributors
(§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

The core scope limit is built into the measure: the Gauge captures what
audiences watch through a television screen, not all viewing on phones,
computers, tablets, or other devices (§ About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. Nielsen says YouTube captured 13.4% of U.S. TV watch-time in July
   2025, marking its sixth consecutive month as the top media
   distributor in the Gauge (§ Introduction). [🤖]
2. The article says YouTube's 13.4% share put it 4.0 points ahead of
   second-place Disney and constituted the largest lead by any media
   company since the Media Distributor Gauge began in November 2023
   (§ Introduction). [🤖]
3. Nielsen frames this as a summer streaming high within TV-screen
   viewing specifically, not as a general statement about all YouTube
   use across devices or countries (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge).
   [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

This is an `industry_report`, not peer-reviewed scholarship. It can
support a narrow descriptive claim about U.S. television-screen share,
but not causal claims or general claims about YouTube's overall global
attention footprint (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

The Gauge is U.S.-only and television-screen-only. It should not be
treated as a measure of total YouTube use on mobile, desktop, or all
streaming devices combined (§ About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

Because the article is a monthly news-style release about one interval,
it is most useful as a dated snapshot rather than as a stable long-run
series in its own right (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This source is best used as a July 2025 U.S. television-viewing
baseline. It is informative when the manuscript specifically needs a
recent U.S. TV-screen share, but it should not be generalized into a
claim that YouTube dominates all viewing environments (§ Introduction; § About.The.Gauge).
[🤖]
