## Methods

The documentation describes provider-side product rules rather than a
transparent research method. Its value is practical: it tells researchers
what SocialBlade says it tracks, how frequently it refreshes data, and
how historical depth is limited inside the product bundle (§ channels-track;
§ update-frequency; § historical-data). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. SocialBlade says channel tracking is selective rather than
   exhaustive: channels are tracked partly because they are popular and
   partly because users searched for them on SocialBlade (§ channels-track).
   [🤖]
2. SocialBlade's update FAQ says popular profiles may refresh
   throughout the day, but every profile is targeted for at least daily
   freshness (§ update-frequency). [🤖]
3. SocialBlade's subscription documentation says historical data begin
   only from the day a profile first enters the SocialBlade system, and
   that YouTube historical depth is limited to a maximum of three years
   in that product context (§ historical-data). [🤖]
