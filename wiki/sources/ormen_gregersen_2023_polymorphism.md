# Ørmen and Gregersen (2023) - Institutional Polymorphism on YouTube

## Full Citation

Ørmen, Jacob, and Andreas Gregersen. 2023. "Institutional Polymorphism: Diversification of Content and Monetization Strategies on YouTube." *Television & New Media* 24(4): 432-451. https://doi.org/10.1177/15274764221110198

## One-Sentence Contribution

Using mixed quantitative and qualitative analysis of 503 YouTube channels, the paper argues that platform pressures generate diversification rather than simple convergence in content and monetization strategies (pp. 432, 444). [✓]

## Research Question

To what extent do YouTube channels work toward specific content niches, how do they monetize their presence on the platform, and how are those differences manifested in organizational identity and strategic response? (pp. 435-436) [✓]

## Data, Sample, Geography, and Period

- **Data:** API-derived channel data, human-coded content and monetization variables, and case-based qualitative analysis of selected channels (pp. 434-438, 444-447). [🤖]
- **Sample:** An initial sampling frame of 25,000 channels from daily empty-search API queries was narrowed through purposive criteria to a final sample of 503 active channels; monetization comparisons then focused on channels with at least 1,000 subscribers (pp. 436-443). [🤖]
- **Geography:** Global rather than country-bounded, with channel examples spanning multiple regions and organizational types (pp. 436-447). [🤖]
- **Period:** The API sampling frame covers channels discovered from 2006 to 2019, and the monetization coding reflects conditions at the time of coding in summer 2019 (pp. 436-443). [🤖]

## Methods

The paper combines a human-coded quantitative content analysis of approximately 500 channels with a case-based qualitative analysis that builds a four-part typology of strategic responses to platform pressure: conformity, compromise, avoidance, and defiance (pp. 434-445). [🤖]

## Main Findings

1. The evidence supports diversification rather than convergence: YouTube channels do not simply comply with platform pressure but display institutional polymorphism in content and monetization strategies (pp. 432, 444). [✓]
2. New media channels dominate the sample and are substantially more heterogeneous than legacy media or general-industry channels, spanning all 19 topical categories and changing subject matter more often over time (pp. 440-441). [🤖]
3. Among channels above the monetization threshold, legacy media tend to rely on ads alone, general-industry channels often avoid monetizing on-platform, and new media channels show the widest mix of ads, combined, alternative, and no monetization (pp. 443-444). [🤖]
4. The case-based analysis identifies four strategic response types - conformity, compromise, avoidance, and defiance - showing that organizational identity mediates how channels respond to platform dependence (pp. 444-447). [🤖]

## Limitations / Scope Conditions

The authors stress that outsiders cannot draw a truly random sample of YouTube channels through the API, and that YouTube's official content categories are too vague and incomplete to serve as reliable analytic labels on their own (pp. 436-437). [✓]

The final sample is purposive rather than population-representative, which makes it stronger for mapping strategic variety than for estimating platform-wide frequencies (pp. 436-437). [🤖]

Because watch time is not publicly available, monetization comparisons rely on subscriber thresholds and visible channel signals rather than full internal eligibility or payout data (p. 443). [🤖]

## Temporal Scope

This is a pre-pandemic, pre-Shorts, summer-2019 snapshot of channel strategy. It is best read as evidence about platform-era organizational adaptation before the later expansion of Shorts, shopping, and other post-2020 monetization features (pp. 436-443). [🤖]

## Key Quotes

> "we find greater support for diversification, that is, polymorphism, than concentration" (p. 432). [✓]

> "there is no way (for outsiders) to draw a truly random sample of YouTube's channel population" (p. 436). [✓]
