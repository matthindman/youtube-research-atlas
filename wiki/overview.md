# YouTube Scholarship Atlas — Overview

This page is the executive synthesis of the atlas. It is
intentionally short: a reader arriving here should be able to
understand, in 5–10 minutes, what the YouTube Census project knows
about the field, what the main open questions are, and where to
drill in for detail.

**Status:** scaffolded only. Awaiting Phase 1 seed corpus ingest.
This page will be generated from theme pages and the claim registry
after Phase 1 completes and the quality calibration pause is
resolved.

## How to read the atlas

- **Theme pages** (`wiki/themes/`) synthesize across sources within
  a topic area. Start here for any cross-source question.
- **Source cards** (`wiki/sources/`) summarize individual papers and
  reports. Start here for any "what does <paper> actually say?"
  question.
- **Debate pages** (`wiki/debates/`) track contested claims. Start
  here for any "where does the literature disagree, and why?"
  question.
- **Paper dossiers** (`wiki/papers/`) collect literature, internal
  reasoning, and draft prose for our own manuscripts. Start here
  when actively drafting.
- **Method pages** (`wiki/methods/`) document methodological
  approaches and their trade-offs.
- **Registries** (`data/`) index sources, cross-cutting claims, and
  the controlled vocabulary.

## Provenance rules (reader shorthand)

- `[🤖]` — machine extracted, not yet human-verified.
- `[✓]` — human-verified against the source.
- `[✓✓]` — verified and safe for manuscript use.
- `[NEEDS CITATION]` — unsupported, do not cite.
- `[LIT]` / `[PROJECT]` / `[DRAFT]` — in paper dossiers only; see
  `docs/review-policy.md`.

Do not quote anything into a manuscript unless the claim is at
`[✓]` or better and the page is at `citation-checked` or better.
